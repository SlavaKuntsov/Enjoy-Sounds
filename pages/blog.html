In the world of music, vinyl records have long become not just a carrier of music, but also a real work of art. The modern design of record covers is not only a simple design element, but also an object of true admiration and love among collectors.

Vinyl record covers can be a real work of art, not only because of the genius of the design, but also because they have become an integral part of the musical product. Many albums released on vinyl were decorated by famous artists. One of the most famous covers in the history of vinyl records is the album of The Beatles "Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band", designed by artist Peter Blake.

Interesting Fact!

Songs that are located closer to the center of the vinyl record sound better than songs on the outer edges. Why is this happening? The rotation speed of the vinyl record is the same on the first track and on the last, but the track length on the first track is 2-3 times longer than on the last. This means that an equal length accommodates a greater amount of sound information in the form of protrusions and depressions, which in turn increases the number of possible noises and minor inaccuracies that would remain unnoticed at a shorter length (which happens closer to the center of the vinyl record, where the playing radius is smaller).
